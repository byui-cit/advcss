Give-N-Go Part 1

So I was introduced to a cool project a while ago. Give-N-Go.co. It shares the same goals of the CSS Zen Garden I believe, to show what can be accomplished with HTML, CSS, and in some cases javascript. Basically the authors will first spend some time at dribbble.com (a place for graphic artists to post examples of their work) until they find something they like. Then they create an html/css version of the graphic they find. The best ones are showcased on the Give-N-Go site. We are going to participate!

Activity

For this activity you will choose a graphic from dribbble.com that you will then turn into a codepen as html/sass/js.

Step 01:  Begin this activity by choosing a graphic from dribbble.com Try to keep it simple, but interesting. I recommend typing "animated" or "ui" into the search box. UI related elements will usually be a bit simpler to build. Good projects should one or more of the following: css shapes, svg, animations, transitions, transforms, or interesting layouts.
