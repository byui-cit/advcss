Create a Walkthrough

It's said that one of the best ways to learn something is to have to teach it. This week you had the opportunity to learn about creating shapes with CSS. To help the concepts you were introduced to this week stick, I would like to give you the opportunity to teach someone else about them. You need to create a walkthrough instructing how to create a specific shape with css and borders, or how to create a repeating background with a gradient.

Activity

Step 1:   Complete the ponder activities for this module, then decide whether you would like to base your walkthrough on a shape (border-radius) or a background (gradients). If you decide on a shape with border radius, you might refer back to the CSS-Tricks: Shapes of CSS article for ideas. Or you could make a new shape of your own design.

If you decide on a repeating background with gradients...then Lea Verou has a lot of examples for inspiration.

Step 2:   Study your example until you understand what each line is doing, then prepare your code and script for your walkthrough and create a screencast of you teaching the material you have prepared. Try to keep your screencast to around 3 minutes. Post your screencast to the class discussion. If you don't have some screencast creation software that you already use I've had pretty good success with Screencast-o-matic

Completion

  • Screencast shows understanding of the topic.
  • Screencast includes clear examples.
  • Student narration is clear and understandable.
