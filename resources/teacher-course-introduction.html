WDD 331 Course Introduction

Description

This course is designed in a way that tries to recognize that most of the specific technologies we could study related to CSS will change. The placement of the first outcome is not random.

It is the most important thing the students could gain from this course.

This course is also repeatable. The course design is also to accommodate that. The first four weeks are set, they will always be the same thing:

  1. Review and introduction
  2. Selectors
  3. SCSS
  4. Zen Garden introduction

Also during those first four weeks the students will be asked to pay close attention to every website they visit. They should take note of anything that catches their attention. They are looking for things that the builder of the site has done that they think are cool and that they would be interested in learning how to replicate. The ideas can be submitted at anytime during the first 4 weeks to a shared Trello board, but each student must submit 3-4 total by the end of week 4. They will submit the links in Ilearn at that time as well to aid the instructor in grading this participation.

During weeks 5-12 of the semester items will be chosen from the list, assigned a complexity and time, and the class will learn to re-create the example. When one is done another will be chosen. These can take anywhere from a few days to up to three weeks.

Spend a few minutes each week highlighting some of the ideas to build excitement and remind the students they are supposed to be paying attention to the sites they visit.

As you start a new example begin by helping the students to deconstruct the problem. Ideally this will result in the students having a list of technologies that they will need to study in order to solve the problem, and a rough blueprint for how to proceed. The instructor can give them some resources to help with this, but the students should be encouraged to find their own as well and to share what they learn with the rest of the class.

You can use this: Collection of learning modules as a starting point for resources.

Repeating students

For students taking the course for a second time, use them to help curate the ideas being submitted during the first four weeks. They can help you prioritize the list, and get an initial estimate of complexity. They are welcome to submit their own examples as well. You could even get them started on solving one of the examples early if they need more to do. You could perhaps have them share their solution with the rest of the class as an introduction to week5.

Best Practices

Our goal is not to just solve the problems, but solve them using best practices as well. As you select examples to work on watch for opportunities to insert things from the following list as additional requirements as needed:

  • Architecture and organization: BEM, Atomic Design, OOCSS, CUBE, etc
  • Debugging and Optimization techniques

Course Setup

You will need to create a Microsoft Team for your section, and add all of your students. If you would prefer Slack that is fine as well. You will also need to create a Trello board and add the students to that as well. Post the link to the board in the messaging system you have chosen.

Grading

Each week the students will be required to complete a reflection on what they learned that week, along with any questions or requests for feedback from the instructor. These will make up 30% of the student's final grade. The other 70% of the grade will come from a self-evaluation of themselves on the course Outcomes. This can be done in one of two ways.

  1. The student can schedule a meeting with the instructor for a face-to-face meeting during the last week of the semester. This is done as an interview where the student offers a claim of mastery, and the instructor challenges as needed. The student should answer the challenges by showing examples of their work that they feel justify their claim.
  2. The student can prepare a short video of themselves giving their self-evaluation along with examples of the work they have completed over the semester that back up their claims. The links to the work should be submitted with the review as a portfolio.
